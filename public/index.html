Regional voice
The network reaches across three distinct communities — Northern, Central, and Southern — each moved by different things. Tune your message to the audience you're reaching.
Northern Reach — San Luis Obispo County
Slower, rustic, outdoors- and Cal Poly–centered · “SLO” living

Unhurried and deeply tied to the outdoors, agriculture, and Cal Poly. People value a balanced, down-to-earth life and loyalty to local institutions.

Who you're reaching
  • Slow-paced Central Coast locals — skeptical of big-city infrastructure, fiercely loyal to local institutions; value relational care over flashy corporate models.
  • Active outdoor enthusiasts & students — focused on sports medicine, PT, and rapid recovery to get back to hiking, surfing, and cycling.
Tone that works
Down-to-earth, relational, unhurried.
Central Core — Santa Barbara County
High-end and leisure-oriented, under real cost-of-living pressure

A community balancing an affluent, wellness-focused lifestyle with a hyper-expensive housing market. They value wellness, prestige, and deep community roots.

Who you're reaching
  • Montecito / Hope Ranch retirees & wealthy remote workers — want premium, hyper-coordinated, concierge care; expect seamless digital tools but high-touch service and privacy.
  • UCSB / Isla Vista / Goleta tech & academic transplants — tech-forward and independent; value fast clinical turnarounds, telehealth, and on-demand urgent care.
  • Santa Maria / Lompoc working class — a large, often-overlooked agriculture & aerospace segment; family-centric, frequently bilingual (Spanish/English), facing geographic and economic barriers to care.
Tone that works
Polished but warm — and never blind to access and affordability.
Southern Reach — Ventura County
Transition zone where the Central Coast meets greater LA

Where the relaxed coast meets fast-paced, commuter-heavy LA energy. Time is constrained and convenience is king.

Who you're reaching
  • “LA spillover” commuters — balancing intense work schedules with coastal living; hyper-focused on efficiency, convenience, and evening/weekend availability.
  • Coastal suburban families — navigating multi-generational care; think school sports physicals, weekend urgent care, and reliable insurance coverage.
Tone that works
Efficient, convenient, family-practical.

About this tool

This tool role-plays six fictional Santa Barbara–area donor personas built from Cottage Health Foundation audience research and the Foundation’s Voice of Philanthropy work. When you paste copy, pull a web page, or upload a PDF or image, each persona reacts in character and gives it a fit score. Every reaction is filtered through the same source material: the Foundation’s brand-voice calibration (the workshop sliders), the Tier 1 and Tier 2 word banks, the words-and-phrases-to-avoid list, and the photography principles. That’s why it rewards on-voice language, flags off-voice language, and looks for warmth, hope, local connection, and donor empowerment — it’s measuring your draft against a defined, consistent voice rather than one person’s opinion.

It’s built for evaluation and curation, not creation — a fast gut-check to pressure-test and refine messaging before it goes out, not a replacement for writing or for real conversations with donors. The personas are AI role-play and their feedback is directional, so the tool isn’t perfect and will sometimes get things wrong. Treat its scores and suggestions as a thoughtful second opinion, then use your own judgment.

How it weighs things. Not every signal counts equally. The score and the order in which fixes are surfaced follow this priority, set with the Foundation — most important first:

  1. Critical Inspiration over obligation — hope and possibility, never guilt or pressure
  2. Critical Community focus, outside-in — inclusive “we / our / us,” benefit to the community, not the institution
  3. High Warmth & humanity
  4. High Donor empowerment
  5. High Local relevance (coast to valley)
  6. High Avoids do-not-use language
  7. High Relationship-building over the ask
  8. High On-brand voice overall
  9. Medium Tier 1 core vocabulary
  10. Medium Tier 2 word banks
  11. Medium Clarity & readability

Critical criteria move the score the most; High criteria meaningfully raise or lower it; Medium criteria nudge it. The heat map shows these as columns so you can see exactly where a piece is strong or weak.

Beta version. Please report any errors and share suggestions to help us improve the tool.
